NON-DISCLOSURE AGREEMENT

BACKGROUND

  1. The Disclosing Party is engaged in the business of providing business process outsourcing services for various functions ("Business").
  2. The Disclosing Party and the Receiving Party are interested in discussing a possible business association or arrangement. The Parties acknowledge that during the course of discussions between them, the Receiving Party shall become privy to certain Confidential Information (defined hereinafter) relating to the Disclosing Party and the Receiving Party has agreed to be bound by the non-disclosure provisions of this Agreement to govern the use and disclosure of the Confidential Information.
  3. In consideration of the mutual promises and agreements between the Parties hereto, the Parties have agreed to enter into this Agreement to govern the terms and conditions of their association.

NOW THEREFORE IT IS HEREBY AGREED BY AND AMONGST THE PARTIES AS UNDER:

  1. DEFINITION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION
    1. For the purpose of this Agreement, the term "Confidential Information" shall mean such information relating to the Disclosing Party, its subsidiaries or affiliates, its customers, directors, founders, owners, employees, consultants or associates, as the Disclosing Party may from time to time provide to the Receiving Party under or relating to this Agreement including all information communicated in writing or orally relating to business affairs, any technical data, or know-how, including but not limited to, that which is or relates to:
    2. Inventions, ideas, processes, research, formats, formulas, human-readable code on any media, object code, data, programs, specifications, other works of authorship, improvements, discoveries, developments, designs, and techniques;
    3. Business operating model, product plans, products, terms and scope of services, customers, markets, software, developments, inventions, processes, designs, drawings, engineering, hardware configuration information;
    1.1.1 Inventions, ideas, processes, research, formats, formulas, human-readable code on any media, object code, data, programs, specifications, other works of authorship, improvements, discoveries, developments, designs, and techniques; 1.1.2 Business operating model, product plans, products, terms and scope of services, customers, markets, software, developments, inventions, processes, designs, drawings, engineering, hardware configuration information; 1.1.3 Marketing or finances of the company in any form, customer information, business plans and strategies, price or fee lists, and market studies; 1.1.4 Contracts and client databases, computer models, software, applications and programs, research records, statistical methods of doing business, customers, finances, strategic and marketing plans, employee details; 1.1.5 Personal information such as phone numbers, bank account details, transaction details, or without restriction, any other personal information; which may be disclosed under this agreement to the Receiving Party, by the Disclosing Party, its subsidiaries or affiliates, its customers, directors, founders, owners, employees, consultants, or associates, and which is not in the public domain. 1.2 For the purpose of this Agreement, the term "Confidential Information" shall mean such information relating to the Disclosing Party, its subsidiaries or affiliates, its customers, directors, founders, owners, employees, consultants or associates, as the Disclosing Party may from time to time provide to the Receiving Party under or relating to this Agreement including all information communicated in writing or orally relating to business affairs, any technical data, or know-how, including but not limited to, that which is or relates to: 1.2.1 The Receiving Party can show was in the public domain prior to the time of the communication thereof to Receiving Party by the Disclosing Party; 1.2.2 Entered the public domain subsequent to the time of the Disclosing Party’s communication thereof to     the Receiving Party other than as a result of a breach of this Agreement by Receiving Party; or 1.2.3 The Disclosing Party has authorized the Receiving Party, in writing, to disclose (but only to the extent of such authorization).
  2. NON-DISCLOSURE AND CONFIDENTIALITY 2.1 The Receiving Party agrees and undertakes: 2.1.1 That the Receiving Party shall not, without the prior written permission of the Disclosing Party, directly or indirectly disclose or cause to be disclosed any Confidential Information to any third party, except to the officers, employees, agents, or representatives of the Receiving Party or the Receiving Party’s Affiliates (collectively the “Representatives”), who, in each case, have a need to know any suchConfidential Information for purposes of the implementation and performance by the Receiving                 Party of its obligations pursuant to this Agreement, and who will use the Confidential Information provided by the Disclosing Party only for such limited purposes; 2.1.2 Not duplicate or incorporate the Confidential Information into its own records or databases; 2.1.3 That the Receiving Party shall protect the Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party by using at least the same degree of care, which shall in no event be less than reasonable care, as the Receiving Party uses to protect its own confidential information of like nature and ensure that any unauthorized disclosure thereof does not occur;2.1.4 That the Receiving Party shall promptly inform the Disclosing Party of any accidental disclosure of Confidential Information by itself or its Representatives and shall take all steps, together with the Disclosing Party, to retrieve and protect the Confidential Information. 2.2 In the event that the Receiving Party is an entity, and the Confidential Information is of a personal or sensitive nature, the Receiving Party shall ensure that it has appropriate internal policies (‘Policies’) in place to protect the Confidential Information shared hereunder. Copies of the Policies shall be provided to the Disclosing Party upon request (however provision of such Policies shall not constitute Disclosing Party’s acceptance of the reasonableness of such Policies); and the Receiving Party shall otherwise cooperate with the Disclosing Party in confirming that the Policies are sufficient to maintain the Confidential Information in accordance with Applicable Law (as defined in Clause 8 hereunder) and industry standard methods of protection. 2.3 Except to the extent required by Applicable Law, neither Party nor any of its Representatives will, without the prior written consent of the other Party, disclose to any person the fact that Confidential Information has been     made available to Receiving Party or its Representatives, that the Parties are considering or have entered into the any association or arrangement, that discussions or negotiations between the Parties are taking or have taken place concerning such associations or arrangement, or any term, condition or other fact relating to the association or arrangement or such discussions or negotiations, including the status thereof. 2.4 The Receiving Party may use the Confidential Information only for internal evaluation purposes or in support of tasks requested in writing by an authorized representative of the Disclosing Party. The Receiving Party shall neither commercialize the Confidential Information nor use it for any other purposes. 2.5 Neither Party shall alter or remove from any Confidential Information any proprietary rights legend, copyright    notice, trademark or trade secret legend, or any other mark identifying the material as Confidential Information. 2.6 The Receiving Party shall not, except as and to the extent required, make any copies or reproduce the Confidential Information. Such copies or reproductions shall be subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement and the Receiving Party shall take such steps as are necessary to restrict access to and protect the confidentiality of such copies or reproductions of the Confidential Information. 2.7 The Receiving Party shall strictly adhere to the provisions mentioned in Clauses 2.1 to 2.6 except: 2.7.1 To the extent that such Confidential Information is already in the public domain, other than by breach of this Agreement; 2.7.2 To the extent that such Confidential Information is required to be disclosed by any applicable law or any applicable regulatory requirements or by any regulatory body to whose jurisdiction the Receiving Party is subject or with whose instructions it is customary to comply under notice to the Disclosing Party; 2.7.3 In so far as it is disclosed to the employees, directors, partner, financiers or professional advisers of the Receiving Party, provided that the Receiving Party shall procure that such persons treat such Confidential Information as confidential; and 2.7.4 To the extent that any of such Confidential Information was previously known or already in the lawful possession of the Receiving Party, prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party.
  3. DATA PROTECTION, SECURITY, AND PRIVACY 3.1 The Receiving Party shall implement and maintain commercially reasonable data security measures designed to prevent unauthorized access to Confidential Information and shall periodically review and update such measures and maintain the same in accordance with no less than industry-standard methods of protection. Without limiting its obligations otherwise set forth herein, the Receiving Party shall comply with all Applicable                 Laws relating to the Receiving Party’s use, storage, transmission, and disclosure of Confidential Information (including without limitation applicable consumer protection and privacy laws).   3.2 The Receiving Party shall immediately notify the Disclosing Party upon Receiving Party’s discovery of:   3.2.1 Any loss, unauthorized disclosure, or unauthorized use of the Confidential Information; or 3.2.2 Any access, acquisition, or misappropriation of Confidential Information by third parties (or attempted access, acquisition, or misappropriation), including any intrusion into the computer system(s) used by Recipient to access or store Confidential Information or theft of physical documents; or 3.2.3 Any other disclosure of Confidential Information in violation of this Agreement or in breach of the Receiving Party’s security measures (collectively, a “Security Breach”). 3.3 In the event of a Security Breach, the Receiving Party shall take such steps (at the Receiving Party’s sole cost and expense) as are necessary to: 3.3.1 Promptly mitigate the effects of such disclosure and prevent a recurrence thereof, and 3.3.2 Comply with Applicable Laws relating to such disclosure. 3.4 The Receiving Party shall indemnify and defend the Disclosing Party and hold the Disclosing Party harmless from and against any and all claims or losses arising from a Security Breach or other unauthorized disclosure or unauthorized use of Confidential Information, including, without limitation, any costs related to notifying customers, regulators or third parties and any attorneys’ fees, and the Receiving Party shall be responsible for all damages, costs, liabilities, or penalties incurred in connection with any such disclosure. The Receiving Party agrees to consult with the Disclosing Party prior to sending any notification(s) of security breach required under applicable privacy laws relating to Confidential Personal Information to the customers of the Disclosing Party.
